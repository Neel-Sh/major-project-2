Correction vs. Connection: The Lasting Impacts of Educator Feedback

Final project presenting snapshots, a full essay, and sources showing that feedback isn’t just about correction—it’s about connection. Thoughtful, process‑based comments nurture motivation and identity; harsh, vague remarks leave lasting harm.

Read the Essay View Sources

Quick Summary

Feedback is never neutral. Supportive, process‑focused comments build resilience and self‑regulated learning; vague or harsh criticism can erode confidence and silence voices. Research by Sherma (2025), Truax (2018), and Zhu et al. (2024) underlines these long‑term effects.
