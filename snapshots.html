Snapshot A — Graded in Red

Context: First advanced English course; heavy red‑ink criticism.

  • Excitement turned to self‑doubt after receiving a bold red “C”.
  • Effort felt invalidated; later “A” felt like it belonged to her father, not her.
  • Takeaway: Vague, harsh feedback eroded confidence and identity as a writer.

Snapshot B — Tutoring Transformation

Context: One‑on‑one tutoring with patient guidance and breaks.

  • Reading together reframed writing as a rewarding challenge.
  • Motivation and enjoyment increased; sessions became something to anticipate.
  • Takeaway: Personalized, process‑focused feedback nurtured growth mindset and persistence.

Snapshot C — Strategy Over Scores

Context: How feedback channels students toward self‑regulated learning (SRL).

  • Directive, scaffolded comments lead to planning, revising, and monitoring habits.
  • Criticism without guidance discourages strategy use despite immediate grades.
  • Takeaway: Long‑term gains come from SRL strategies that feedback can cultivate.
